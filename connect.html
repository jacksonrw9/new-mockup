Connecting Your Instruments to Our Platform

Simple Instrument Onboarding with Pre-Built Connectors

Connect scientific instruments using our simple five-step process and automatically standardizing multi-vendor protocols, automatically communicating in your instrument's native protocols, capturing sample data, and feeding it to our Data Formatting Engine for standardization.

Learn More About Automating Data Formatting

Vendor Agnostic Platform

If your facility uses multiple vendors for the same instrument class, everything appears unified in our Connected Plant interface – one consistent view for remote operation, data review, and monitoring activity across all your equipment.

Built for Legacy and Modern Instruments Alike

We support everything from workhorse legacy instruments with limited connectivity to modern devices with built-in WiFi - our microservices approach bridges the compatibility gap for complete integration.

Protection Against Data Loss

Continuous data capture is maintained even during network failures and external interruptions. Your critical analytical data is automatically preserved and synchronized once connectivity is restored.

Enable Continuous Data Capture and Remote Operation

Connecting your instruments enables continuous monitoring of all sample data and device activity while allowing operators to remotely start, stop, and monitor operations without leaving their computer – eliminating printouts and manual processes like transcription and data entry.

Heart Beat Differentiation

Real-time heartbeat monitoring, not APIs.

We capture data second-by-second through continuous connections, detecting offline instruments immediately and maintaining complete visibility even during idle periods. Unlike API solutions that only pull data at scheduled intervals, our approach ensures no data loss and provides complete operational context.

Supported Instruments

We currently support multi-vendor air particle counters, microbial samplers, pH meters, balances, spectrometers, flow cytometers, zeta potential analyzers, and many other analytical scientific instruments in some of the biggest cleanroom level production sites in the world.
