How Remotely Operating Air Particle Counters and Automating Data Collection Cuts Manufacturing Costs and Increase Lab Tech Productivity

If your lab techs no longer had to physically operate air particle counters to take samples…

No longer needed to retrieve paper print outs from these instruments to copy them with a pen and paper…

And never had to manually input air particle counter results into a database…

What would happen?

An analysis of our current customers' data showed that remotely operating their air particle counters and completely automating data collection resulted in substantial cost savings and big productivity gains for their lab technicians.

By eliminating the need for second-person review and all paper printouts.

Drastically reducing human errors associated with hand transferring data from paper to a database.

And for one of the five largest pharmaceutical manufacturers globally…

These improvements led to a 40% productivity boost among lab technicians and allowed the production line to reduce the required number of technicians per air particle counter from 1.8 to 1.

Up until now most of the life sciences industry has remained dependent on many pen and paper processes for all data related to air particle counters….

And accepted the inherent risks and costs associated with them.

Mainly because of the unique set of challenges present in life science manufacturing: ensuring compliance with strict regulatory standards, minimizing production line downtime, and implementing a seamless, paperless data collection process that integrates with their existing LIMS systems without disrupting validated workflows.

While dealing with legacy instruments that have limited connectivity and network capabilities.

But now with Connected Plant, we've made operation and data collection quite simple…

And 100% 21 CFR Part 11 compliant.

That also has the capability to integrate with your current LIMS solution.

Without overhauling your SOPs…

And this works with whichever air particle counter you're operating…

Whether you're relying on Met One air particle counters…

Climet, Aerotrak, or Lighthouse…

Or even if you have a mix of air particle counters from multiple brands…

We've got you covered.
